# Briarmont Rental Covenant

This covenant governs the use of residential and business rental units within Briarmont. The Briarmont Rental Covenant exists to define the obligations of tenants, clarify how rental spaces are managed, and establish the standards that apply to leased properties.

This rental covenant also serves as a binding contract between tenants, their occupants and Briarmont. Furthermore, all tenants and occupants remain subject to the Briarmont Community Covenant, Roleplay Guidelines, and Community Standards, in addition to any applicable Linden Lab policies.

Initiation of a lease within Briarmont indicates agreement to follow the terms outlined within this rental covenant.


## 1. Covenant Terms, Eligibility, and Tenant Limits

For the remainder of this document, the following terms will be used to refer to the different parties that may become involved:

**Briarmont**/**Briamont Staff** refers to the broader community which offers the units described in this section for lease, their relevant staff, and administrators.
**Tenants** refers to the person(s) responsible for the maintainence of an active lease in Briarmont. A *Primary tenant* is the tenant that first started the lease, *subtenants* are person(s) added as tenants by the Primary tenant.
**Occupant(s)** refers to the person(s) residing within the tenant's rental unit, are not directly responsible for the maintainence of the rental unit, however still rez personal belongings and objects within the unit. 
**Guests** refers to the person(s) visiting the unit the tenants and their occupant(s) are actively leasing in Briarmont. Guests refers only to Non-roleplay visitors within this context, however members of Briarmont's community may also be considered guests in a rental unit.
**To rent** / **renting** refers to maintaining an active lease for a rental unit through weekly payments by the primary tenant and any listed subtenant(s).
**Rental unit** refers to any property type listed in Section 2: Residential and Business Use within Briarmont that is available for rent.
**Active lease** refers to permission granted to the primary tenant, subtenant(s), and their occupant(s) to rez objects and personal property within the bounds of the rental unit’s parcel.

To rent in Briarmont, tenants and their occupant(s) must meet the community's current access requirements, including the minimum account age described in the Community Standards.

Renting in Briarmont does not require a defined character for Roleplay. Non-roleplay tenants may still be eligible to rent so long as the tenants and their occupant(s) comply with Briarmont's broader community standards and operational requirements.

Briarmont offers a Discord server as part of its wider community communication methods. Discord is not required in order to rent within Briarmont, however, it may often be the primary platform where announcements, support information, or community updates are shared.


## 2. Residential and Business Use

As of (Final Ver. Month, Year), Briarmont offers a range of rental units for residential and commercial (business) use. This section outlines each available unit type, its intended use, and its applicable tenant, subtenant, and occupancy limits.

### 2A. Residential Uses

Briarmont offers the following type of residential units for lease:

 - Single Family Homes
 - Apartments
 - Student Dormitories (Dorms)

Residential units are intended to function as private living spaces within the broader Briarmont environment.

Residential Homes and Apartments may have one primary tenant plus up to two subtenants, unless otherwise stated. The primary tenant will be the sole point of contact regarding all rental related requests and issues. Responsibility for maintaining the lease rests with the primary tenant, and Briarmont Staff will not mediate disputes between the primary tenant and their subtenant(s) and/or occupant(s).

Dorm rentals are limited to one tenant only, with no subtenants permitted. Dorm leases are subject to a strict 100 LI limit. Dorms adjustments are made at Briarmont Staff's discretion.

Guests are permitted within a tenant’s rental unit at the tenant’s discretion. Guests in shared or common areas must comply with Briarmont’s community standards and operational requirements. Tenants and occupant(s) are responsible for the conduct of their guests and for ensuring their guests are informed of and comply with all applicable standards and requirements.

Briarmont Staff reserve the right to determine occupancy limits for any rental unit based on the intended use of the property.


### 2B: Business Uses

Briarmont offers the following type of business units for lease:

 - Storefronts
 - Small to medium sized Eateries
 - Nightlife venues

Business rental units are not limited to the listed uses and the unit names are used as a reference to their size. 

Commencement of a business rental lease, does not guarantee the proposed use will automatically be approved. 

Business rentals are limited, selectively approved, and subject to staff authorization. Approval is required before a business rental may be used for any public-facing commercial or roleplay purpose. 

Approved business rentals may be subject to additional venue-specific expectations depending on their intended use, visibility, and role within the town.

The sale of L$ goods or services through a Briarmont rental is not generally permitted unless explicitly approved by staff. Any such arrangements are negotiated by Briarmont and are not initiated through unsolicited outside proposals.

## 4. LI Overages, Adjustments, and Object Returns

LI limits are part of the rental agreement and are expected to be respected.

If a tenant exceeds their agreed LI limit, Briarmont Staff may make automatic adjustments to the effective rate or rental time associated with that property in order to account for the overage.

Minor overages may be tolerated temporarily at staff discretion, but repeated, intentional, or egregious LI limit abuse may result in enforcement action.  

Within reason, Briarmont Staff may attempt to contact the primary tenant before taking enforcement action, including requesting that LI usage be brought into compliance or that objects be removed or adjusted as necessary. Briarmont Staff retains sole discretion in determining whether an LI overage is minor, excessive, or disruptive enough to require immediate action. If the primary tenant is contacted regarding LI usage or object removal, they are responsible for notifying any subtenant(s) and/or occupant(s) and ensuring all requested removals or adjustments are completed.

Items may be returned to their owners from a rental unit for reasons including, but not limited to, the following:

- Repeated, intentional or egregious LI limit use has required an enforcement action by Briarmont Staff to return the rental unit to its agreed limit.
- The rental unit has been abandoned and is reclaimed by Briarmont Staff.
- The rental unit's lease has been overdue past a reasonable period and is reclaimed by Briarmont Staff.

Briarmont Staff are not responsible for the loss of items, including no-copy objects, when returning objects for any reason.

## 6. Abandonment, Expiration, and Reclamation

Briarmont reserves the right to reclaim rental units that are overdue, abandoned, misused, or no longer being maintained in accordance with community standards.

A rental unit may be considered subject to reclaim when:

- The lease has expired or remains overdue
- The rental unit appears abandoned or functionally unused for an extended period
- The tenants and their occupant(s) have vacated the community or otherwise lost eligibility to remain in Briarmont
- The tenants has materially violated Briarmont's governing covenants and guidelines or rental terms

When a rental unit is reclaimed, items within the space may be returned in the ordinary course of enforcement. See section 4: LI Overages, Adjustments, and Object Returns for policies regarding returned items.

If the primary tenant does not/is unable to renew the lease, any subtenant(s) and/or occupant(s) who wish to remain in the rental unit must contact Briarmont Staff to designate a new primary tenant before the lease expires. Failure to do so may result in unit reclamation upon lease expiration. 

Reclamation decisions are made at staff discretion in the interest of maintaining a functional and well-managed community.


## 7. Rental Conduct and Operational Compliance

Tenants and their occupant(s) are expected to use their rental unit in a way that remains consistent with Briarmont's Community Covenant, Roleplay Guidelines, and Community Standards, including the expectations outlined in this covenant.

Renting a unit does not exempt a tenant, subtenant(s), or their occupant(s) from broader community standards regarding public-facing presentation, visible content, operational requirements, or staff direction.


## 8. Final Rental Authority

Not every rental issue can be anticipated in advance.

Briarmont staff may interpret and enforce this covenant as needed to preserve the setting, manage rental inventory fairly, protect community standards, and support the broader operation of the town.

Where rental issues are not explicitly covered here, staff may issue direction or clarification as necessary, and tenants are expected to comply with those decisions as a condition of continuing to rent within Briarmont.
