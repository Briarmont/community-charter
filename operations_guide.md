# Briarmont Operations Guide

This guide covers the practical, operational standards that support day-to-day life in Briarmont. Unlike the Community Covenant, which defines community behavior and moderation standards, and the Roleplay Guidelines, which describe roleplay conventions, this document focuses on how the community functions in practice.

The Operations Guide is intended to clarify expectations around avatar presentation, public and private spaces, community tools, vehicles and props, groups, events, and other operational matters that help Briarmont remain consistent, grounded, and manageable.

---

## 1. Purpose of This Guide

Briarmont is built as a grounded town setting with a strong emphasis on shared atmosphere, community consistency, and accessible roleplay. To support that environment, some expectations are operational rather than behavioral.

This guide exists to explain those expectations clearly so community members understand how Briarmont functions beyond general conduct and roleplay etiquette.

### Operational Philosophy

Briarmont's operations are guided by a few core principles:

- **Grounded realism**: the setting should feel believable, coherent, and anchored in a contemporary Northern California coastal town.
- **Shared atmosphere**: public-facing presentation, scenes, and systems should contribute to the town's broader environment rather than undermine it.
- **Accessible participation**: community systems should remain as lightweight and minimally intrusive as possible while still supporting consistency, moderation, and roleplay function.
- **Consistency over complexity**: when edge cases arise, Briarmont will generally favor clear and consistent standards over highly technical or exception-heavy rules.

Briarmont also operates under a grounded, real-world inspired framework. In-character actions are expected to carry reasonable and consistent consequences within the setting.

## 2. Avatar and Presentation Standards

Briarmont is a grounded town setting built around human-presenting characters.

Community members are expected to use avatars that visually read as human and remain diegetic to the setting. Avatars, bodies, attachments, or presentation choices that are overtly non-human, fantastical, or disruptive to the environment are not permitted in shared community spaces.

This standard is intended to support a town environment where characters feel grounded within the world of Briarmont. Stylization is acceptable, but presentation should still read as a plausible human character in the setting.

Examples of presentation that may be incompatible with Briarmont include:

- fully non-human or feral avatars
- anthro avatars that do not read as human-presenting
- overt fantasy creatures or monster forms
- tails, ears, appendages, or effects that read as non-human rather than cosmetic or diegetic to the setting
- exaggerated novelty attachments or visual effects that break the environment

Minor cosmetic alterations may be considered on a case-by-case basis if they remain grounded, restrained, and clearly integrated into the character as a human-presenting individual within the setting.

Temporary deviations from normal presentation standards may be permitted for staff-approved events, scenes, or scenarios.

Briarmont may support adult, teen, and child characters as part of its broader community environment, but all such characters are still expected to follow the same presentation standards and remain appropriate to the setting.

Staff reserve the right to determine whether an avatar, attachment, or overall presentation is appropriate for Briarmont.

## 3. Region Ratings and Public vs Private Standards

Briarmont operates as a Moderate-compatible community standard in all shared and public-facing spaces.

In practice, this means public-facing scenes, props, animations, and visual presentation should remain consistent with Briarmont’s Community Covenant and appropriate for a broader community environment.

For the purposes of Briarmont operations, a space should be treated as **public-facing** if it is openly accessible, commonly shared, visible to others, part of a public roleplay area, or reasonably enterable by the broader community.

A space may be treated as **restricted-access** or operationally private when access is meaningfully limited through architecture, parcel controls, community tooling, staff policy, or other active restrictions that prevent unqualified community members from reasonably entering or remaining there.

Closed doors alone do not automatically make a scene private. Visibility, accessibility, and access control all matter.

If a scene can be reasonably seen, heard, entered, or stumbled into by others, it should be treated as public-facing. If a venue is intended to operate as age-restricted, character-restricted, or otherwise restricted-access, those limits must be meaningfully enforced in practice.

When uncertainty exists, community members should default to the more public interpretation or seek clarification from staff.

## 4. Character Creation and Onboarding

Roleplaying in Briarmont requires a defined character.

Community members who wish to roleplay in Briarmont are expected to complete the community onboarding process and any required character registration or submission procedures established by staff.

Accounts must be at least 30 days old to enter or participate in Briarmont in any capacity.

This includes:

- reading the Community Covenant
- reading the Roleplay Guidelines
- completing any required onboarding acknowledgments
- creating or registering a character through Briarmont's approved systems
- using any required community tools associated with roleplay participation

Briarmont also requires use of its approved Experience where needed to support scripted interactions, access control, and community systems.

Some limited non-roleplay presence may still be permitted in Briarmont, such as attendance at major public community events or residence within the town as a non-roleplay tenant. However, these exceptions do not remove the requirement to comply with Briarmont's operational standards or use required systems where they are necessary for access and function.

As Briarmont's systems continue to develop, additional onboarding, titling, HUD, registration, or access requirements may be introduced.

## 5. Community HUD and Required Tools

The Briarmont HUD and Briarmont titler are essential and required tools for normal roleplay participation in the community.

These tools support immersive roleplay, character identification, access control, and other core community systems. Community members participating in roleplay are expected to wear and use them as instructed. These tools are intended to remain lightweight and minimally intrusive in support of Briarmont's accessible roleplay environment.

Briarmont may also require use of additional approved systems or tools where needed to support:

- character registration or verification
- access control and age-based restrictions
- event support or staff operations
- roleplay functionality tied to the Briarmont Experience

These tools are not optional for normal roleplay participation. Failure or refusal to use required systems may limit where a community member can go or what activities they may participate in.

The primary exception is within a renter's own private residential space, where a non-roleplay tenant may be permitted to treat the space as their own and may not be required to wear the HUD or titler so long as they are not engaging in roleplay or entering broader community spaces where those systems are required.

## 6. Vehicles, Props, and Rez Expectations

Community members may rez items, vehicles, props, and temporary objects that their character would reasonably have access to when those objects are being used to support an active roleplay scene or practical in-character activity. Rezzed items should reflect what would reasonably exist in a contemporary Northern California coastal town, unless a staff-approved exception applies.

These objects are expected to enrich the scene without creating clutter, disruption, or performance issues.

General expectations include:

- Rez vehicles and props when they are actively part of a scene or needed for practical use.
- Remove or store temporary objects when they are no longer in use.
- Do not leave vehicles, furniture, roleplay objects, or scene setups abandoned in public areas.
- Do not use props or objects in ways that interfere with access, immersion, safety, or performance.

Sim hygiene is a responsibility, not a right. Community members are responsible for cleaning up their own rezzed items when a scene has concluded.

Staff may return, remove, or restrict objects that are abandoned, disruptive, inappropriate to the setting, or harmful to sim safety or performance. Briarmont is not responsible for the loss of rezzed items, including no-copy objects, that are returned or removed in the course of operational enforcement.

Staff have final discretion in determining whether rezzed items are appropriate for the setting and safe for the broader community environment.

---

## 7. Use of Public and Private Spaces

Public spaces in Briarmont are part of the town's shared atmosphere and should be used in a way that remains respectful to immersion and diegetic to the setting.

Scenes, props, and temporary setups in public or shared areas should contribute to the environment rather than undermine it. Rezzed items in any space, whether used by renters or non-renters, are expected to remain appropriate to Briarmont's curated aesthetic and grounded community environment.

Private homes, residences, and clearly personal areas should be respected. Do not enter, use, or disrupt private spaces without permission.

Even in private or residential spaces, visible presentation that spills into the broader community environment may still be subject to staff review if it materially disrupts immersion, setting consistency, or operational standards.

---

## 8. Groups, Tags, and Official Channels

Briarmont may maintain official groups, tags, channels, and communication spaces for operational, moderation, and community purposes.

Community members may be required to:

- use designated community groups or tags in certain contexts
- keep character-related and OOC communication in the correct channels
- follow any published expectations regarding official groups, tags, or communication structure

Discord is not required for roleplay participation, but it is **strongly recommended**. Much of Briarmont's out-of-character planning, announcements, discussion, and support for connected systems may occur there. Some features, announcements, and support systems may be primarily facilitated through Discord.

Additional rules relating to official groups, faction approval, or communication structure may be published as needed.

---

## 9. Parcels and Local Rules

Some parts of Briarmont may be subject to parcel-specific expectations depending on how the space is used.

Community members are expected to comply with any additional local rules that apply to specific parcels, venues, or community-managed spaces.

Residential rentals and tenant-specific obligations are governed separately through the Briarmont Rental Covenant and any parcel-specific rental terms provided at the point of lease.

Where applicable, local parcel rules operate alongside the Community Covenant, Roleplay Guidelines, and this Operations Guide.

---

## 10. Staff Discretion and Operational Contact

Not every operational question can be anticipated in advance.

Briarmont staff may interpret and apply this guide as needed to preserve the setting, maintain consistency, support performance, protect the broader community experience, and issue operational direction when necessary.

This may include clarifications, temporary restrictions, cleanup instructions, access-related decisions, or other situational guidance needed to keep the community functioning smoothly.

Where operational issues are not explicitly covered here, staff may issue direction or clarification as necessary, and community members are expected to comply with those directions.

Briarmont may also provide designated ways to contact staff or submit operational concerns, including in-world staff contact points, website forms, or official communication channels. Community members are encouraged to use those systems when they need assistance, clarification, or review of an operational issue.
